# There is no strict order to follow in the development of an online business
An effective online business needs this framework in place:
[[Visitors → Subscribers → Customers]]
To develop it you can start from anyone of the 3 elements.
*Content creators* often start from the **visitors**. They grow a public. Then to monetize it they start a [[Mailing list]]. They gather their **subscribers**. And then they present them new products or services to convert them in **customers**.
*Freelancers* may start with the **customers**. They find them through personal contacts or word of mouth. Then to systematize the sales process they start an acquisition channel. With it, they bring **visitors** to their websites and convert them in **subscribers** to their [[Mailing list]]. At that point they have the complete framework in place.
*Entrepreneurs* with a clearer business project in mind from the beginning, start by gathering the first **subscribers** (mainly through personal contacts). Then they work on an acquisition channel, to bring **visitors** to their websites. This way they build a bigger [[Mailing list]]. Through it they present their products or services and increase the number of their **customers**.
